<DIV align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; margin-right: 0%; text-indent: 0%; font-size: 10pt; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    <B><FONT style="font-size: 14pt">SPDR Dow Jones Industrial
    Average ETF Trust</FONT></B>
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; margin-right: 0%; text-indent: 0%; font-size: 10pt; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    <B><FONT style="font-size: 14pt">Trust&#160;Overview</FONT></B>
</DIV>

<DIV style="font-size: 17pt; margin-left: 0%; width: 100%;  align: left; border-bottom: 1pt solid #000000"></DIV>

<DIV style="margin-top: 12pt; font-size: 1pt">&nbsp;</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial, Helvetica; color: #000000; background: transparent">

    <FONT style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times">OBJECTIVE:
    </FONT>
</DIV>

<DIV style="margin-top: 6pt; font-size: 1pt">&nbsp;</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; margin-right: 0%; text-indent: 0%; font-size: 10pt; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    The SPDR Dow Jones Industrial Average ETF Trust (the
    &#147;Trust&#148;) is an exchange traded fund designed to
    generally correspond to the price and yield performance of the
    Dow Jones Industrial Average (the &#147;Index&#148;).
</DIV>

<DIV style="margin-top: 12pt; font-size: 1pt">&nbsp;</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial, Helvetica; color: #000000; background: transparent">

    <FONT style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times">STRATEGY:
    </FONT>
</DIV>

<DIV style="margin-top: 6pt; font-size: 1pt">&nbsp;</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; margin-right: 0%; text-indent: 0%; font-size: 10pt; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    To accomplish this, the Trust utilizes a full replication
    approach. With this strategy, all component securities of the
    Index are owned by the Trust in their relative weight. A
    replication management approach should result in low expected
    tracking error of the Trust relative to its benchmark.
</DIV>

<DIV style="margin-top: 12pt; font-size: 1pt">&nbsp;</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt; font-family: Arial, Helvetica; color: #000000; background: transparent">

    <FONT style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times">PERFORMANCE
    OVERVIEW:
    </FONT>
</DIV>

<DIV style="margin-top: 6pt; font-size: 1pt">&nbsp;</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; margin-right: 0%; text-indent: 0%; font-size: 10pt; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    The Trust ended its fiscal year on October&#160;31, 2011 with a
    <FONT style="white-space: nowrap">12-month</FONT>
    total return of 10.17% on net asset value as compared to the
    Index return of 10.39%.
</DIV>

<DIV style="margin-top: 6pt; font-size: 1pt">&nbsp;</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; margin-right: 0%; text-indent: 0%; font-size: 10pt; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    The Trust&#146;s performance reflects the operating expenses of
    the Trust, including brokerage and Trustee fees. The Index
    returns do not reflect fees and expenses of any kind, which
    would have a negative impact on returns.
</DIV>

    On December&#160;22, 2010, the Regulated Investment Company
    Modernization Act of 2010 (the &#147;Act&#148;) was enacted. The
    Act modernizes several of the federal income and excise tax
    provisions related to RICs, and, with certain exceptions, is
    effective for taxable years beginning after December&#160;22,
    2010. Among the changes made are changes to the capital loss
    carryforward rules allowing for capital losses to be carried
    forward indefinitely. Rules in effect previously limit the
    carryforward period to eight years. Capital loss carryforwards
    generated in taxable years beginning after effective date of the
    Act must be fully used before capital loss carryforwards
    generated in taxable years prior to effective date of the Act;
    therefore, under certain circumstances, capital loss
    carryforwards available as of the report date, if any, may
    expire unused.
